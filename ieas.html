History

The Institute started in the year 2012 and it was named Institute of Computer Studies and Tourism. It was primarily established as a response to the market demands and attune to the needs of the Clark Freeport Zone and other employers in the city, thus assuring a better chance at employment. More importantly, as productive members of the community with a higher sense of dignity. The initial program offerings are the Associate Degree in Computer Science, Associate Degree in Information Systems and Associate Degree in Tourism Management. All these courses are two-year courses and registered under the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA). After a year, the institute applied for Government Authority from the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) to elevate the associate degrees to bachelor’s degree; and by virtue of Government Authority in accordance with the pertinent provisions of “Higher Education Act of 1994,” the City College of Angeles was authorized to operate Bachelor Degree courses effective in the Academic Year 2013-2014 for the following programs: BS in Computer Science, BS in Information Systems and BS in Tourism Management. As for the two year-courses, they were no longer continued, except for the Associate in Computer Science which was changed to Associate in Computer Technology. In the Academic Year 2014-2015, an additional program was added which is the Bachelor of Library and Information Science, however, in the same year the BS in Tourism Management separated to another institute which is the Institute of Business and Management. From then the institute name has change to the Institute of Computing Studies and Library Information Science (ICSLIS). The institute have produced six batches of graduates in BS in Computer Science and BS in Information Systems and four batches under the Bachelor of Library and Information Science program. The institute has subjected itself and its three programs for institutional sustainability assessment and ALCUCOA accreditation where it initially passed the candidate status for its three program offerings and is now eyeing to have the Level I ALCUCOA Accreditation this 2022.

Institute Seal

  1. The overlapping circles represent the three programs offered by the institute which are the BSCS, BSIS and BLIS programs. Additionally, it shows the oneness of the institute in attaining and realizing its vision, mission and goals.
  2. The digital circuits represent computing technologies that serve as the foundations and contents of its program curricula’s. It also represent the transfer of knowledge as it forms connections and linkages not just within the institute but also to the outside world.
  3. The book and the quill pen symbolizes knowledge, intellect, information, and wisdom. An open book means being able to grasp new ideas with ease.
  4. The three circles represent the three core value of the City College of Angeles which are: excellence, resiliency and stewardship.

Job Descriptions

Institute Dean

  1. Evaluates the performance of the program coordinators, laboratory coordinators and faculty members.
  2. Conducts regular meetings with program coordinators and/or faculty members for the continuous integration and improvement of academic programs of the Institute.
  3. Presides meetings for the improvement of Institute.
  4. Evaluates and monitors the performance of the faculty and academic staff of the Institute.
  5. Sees that the objectives and requirements of his/her institute are met by the degree programs.
  6. Executes institute-wide academic policies and transmits them to the students and faculty in his/her unit.
  7. Prepares report on all activities of the Institute.
  8. Offers opportunities for faculty development through research undertakings and other faculty development activities.
  9. Reviews and recommends to the proper body faculty matters such as recruitment and hiring.
  10. Meets the program coordinators, at which time reports are received regarding activities of the department and the academic /professional activities of each faculty member.
  11. Oversees the students taking degree programs within his/ her unit.

Program Coordinators

  1. In charge of administration of the of the curricular programs in terms of supervision of instruction and student activities including interview of new students, recommendation of teaching loads of faculty, renewal and evaluation of program, curricula and syllabi
  2. Reports to the Dean and/or VPAA and President of College.
  3. Works closely with the Instructors and Lecturers under his/her team.
  4. Provides course specific advice to students.
  5. Conducts orientation sessions at the course level (in conjunction with Faculty and School activities.)
  6. Oversees administration of student progress.
  7. Partners/Coordinates with community businesses/institutions to facilitate on the job trainings of students.
  8. Performs other functions which may be assigned by the supervisor and/or head of office.

Faculty

  1. Prepares and types course syllabus (which should be submitted prior to the start of each semester), course description, lesson guide of the subjects assigned to him/her.
  2. Prepares instructional design of the day-to-day class sessions.
  3. Utilizes different teaching strategies in his/her classes.
  4. Prepares visual aids, instructional tools to be used for classroom teaching.
  5. Prepares and types proper evaluative tools in evaluating students' performance through formative and summative evaluations such as quizzes, class participation, major examinations, seat work, laboratory activities, checklist or rating scales for examinations.
  6. Checks test papers, computes grade and submits students' records of grades to the Dean's Office on the specified dates of submission.
  7. Checks, records, follows-up and reports students' attendance.
  8. Informs students of their class standing, examination results and midterm and final grades after each examination period.
  9. Monitors students' attendance.
  10. Attends to his/her classes well-prepared regularly and promptly.

Secretary

  1. Prepares and manages correspondence, reports, and documents.
  2. Organizes and coordinates meetings and events.
  3. Prepares and distributes minutes of meetings.
  4. Maintains schedules and calendars.
  5. Implements and maintains office system.
  6. Handles incoming mails and other materials.
  7. Respond to email, office Facebook page messages, and other inquiries.
  8. Operates satellite office equipment.
  9. Performs other functions which may be assigned by the supervisor and/or head of office.
